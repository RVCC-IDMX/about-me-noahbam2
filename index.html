The Picture of Dorian Gray

Gaming industries, Movie production studios, and practically any company who's dipping their toes into realism has most definitely used a technique for creating real world lighting called Raytracing. An article by NVidia put out a simplified way of explaining what raytracing is and what it does for the gaming and movie industry. The article sums up what it means to ray trace by stating that it basically simulates shadows, reflections, refractions, and indirect light using heavy computational power. It takes the camera (which is the view of the scene) and calculates the view ray through one pixel in the image and hits an object directly beaming into the light, which creates the image on screen. The View ray makes interactions with the 3d environment by passing through objects which might be reflective. Some rays might make it through objects; thats semi-transparent or (refractive). Some of the light rays might bounce off an object and back into the camera to produce a reflective material. All internally producing a convincing image.
